Solar




Source: Goodyear ATV

Overview

  • Solar energy is energy that comes from sunlight. Photovoltaic panels or mirrors are used to concentrate solar radiation, which is the light energy emitted by the sun. This energy is used to generate electricity, and can also be used for longer term energy storage by being stored in batteries or in thermal storage.

Sources: Energy.gov - How Does Solar Work,


Benefits

  • Due to the fact that the amount of sunlight that strikes the Earth in an hour and a half is enough to handle the entire world’s energy consumption for a full year, the use of solar technologies to capture sunlight for energy is very prevalent as it is an opportunity to use this energy and direct it towards humans’ energy needs.
    One benefit is cost savings. For residential solar electricity systems, there are often lower monthly utility bills compared with traditional electricity systems.
    Another benefit is the adaptability. Most states have good-to-excellent solar resources, with areas with low solar resources have similar levels to that of countries with highly developed solar photovoltaics such as Germany.
    Furthermore, the amount of solar energy needed is relatively low, as just one hour of noontime summer sun is equal to the annual US electricity demand.
    Another benefit is the environmental benefits. Every kilowatt-hour (kWh) of solar energy that is generated reduces greenhouse gas emissions like CO2, in addition to other dangerous pollutants such as sulfur oxides, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter. Not only that, but solar energy also reduces water consumption.

Sources: Energy.gov - How Does Solar Work, Energy.gov - 5 Benefits of Residential Solar


Limitations

  • Locations with lower sunlight levels may have more difficulty with implementing fully solar generated energy systems. Some major limitations of solar energy are first of all, the availability and amount of sunlight that arrives at the earth's surface varies depending on time of day, location, season of the year, and weather conditions.
    The second limitation is the amount of sunlight reaching a square foot of the earth's surface is relatively small, so a large surface area is necessary to absorb or collect enough energy to be useful. There can also be environmental impacts when constructing utility-scale solar energy facilities. Many factors can also limit the efficiency of solar cells’ ability to generate energy.

Sources: EIA - Solar Energy Explained, SOLAREIS - Solar Energy Environmental Impacts EIA - Solar Performance and Efficiency
Chemical Process

  • Solar energy uses photovoltaic cells, also called solar cells, to generate energy. When light shines on the cell, the light may reflect, absorb, or pass through the cell. The PV cell is composed of semiconductor material; the “semi” means that it can conduct electricity better than an insulator but not as well as a good conductor like a metal. There are several different semiconductor materials used in PV cells.When the semiconductor is exposed to light, it absorbs the light’s energy and transfers it to negatively charged particles in the material called electrons.This extra energy allows the electrons to flow through the material as an electrical current.Some of the most commonly used semiconductor materials for PV cells are silicon, thin film photovoltaics, perovskite solar cells, and organic photovoltaics. In addition, there are also quantum dot solar cells, multijunction solar cells, and concentration photovoltaics.
    Three types of silicon solar cell materials are thin film, monocrystalline, and polycrystalline.

    Silicon is, by far, the most common semiconductor material used in solar cells, representing approximately 95% of the modules sold today. It is also the second most abundant material on Earth (after oxygen) and the most common semiconductor used in computer chips. Crystalline silicon cells are made of silicon atoms connected to one another to form a crystal lattice. This lattice provides an organized structure that makes conversion of light into electricity more efficient. A thin-film solar cell is made by depositing one or more thin layers of PV material on a supporting material such as glass, plastic, or metal. There are two main types of thin-film PV semiconductors on the market today: cadmium telluride (CdTe) and copper indium gallium diselenide (CIGS). Both materials can be deposited directly onto either the front or back of the module surface. Perovskite solar cells are a type of thin-film cell and are named after their characteristic crystal structure. Perovskite cells are built with layers of materials that are printed, coated, or vacuum-deposited onto an underlying support layer, known as the substrate. They are typically easy to assemble and can reach efficiencies similar to crystalline silicon. Organic PV, or OPV, cells are composed of carbon-rich (organic) compounds and can be tailored to enhance a specific function of the PV cell, such as bandgap, transparency, or color. OPV cells are currently only about half as efficient as crystalline silicon cells and have shorter operating lifetimes, but could be less expensive to manufacture in high volumes.

Sources: Energy.gov - Solar Photovoltaic Cell Basics
